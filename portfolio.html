BinWatch — Case Study

My Final Year Project at Universiti Sultan Zainal Abidin (UniSZA) — a smart waste-management platform for hostels and campuses, built with Flutter and Firebase.

The Problem

Hostel bins overflow before cleaners are alerted. Supervisors have no visibility into bin status, and reports are tracked manually on paper — leading to missed pickups, hygiene issues and accountability gaps.

The Solution

BinWatch combines ultrasonic IoT sensors, a Firebase backend, and a Flutter mobile app with three role-based portals. Bins self-report fill levels in real time, supervisors assign tasks, and cleaners receive instant push alerts.

The Impact

Reduces overflow incidents, automates approval workflows, and gives administrators live data and reports for evidence-based decisions on waste-collection schedules and cleaner deployment.

The Smart Bin & Sensor

What you're looking at: a 3D model of the smart bin used in BinWatch. An HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor is fixed under the lid and pings downward — the time-of-flight reading is converted into a fill percentage and pushed to Firebase. The bin auto-rotates so you can see how the sensor is mounted on the underside of the top.

System Architecture

IoT sensors stream fill levels to Firebase Realtime Database. Firestore stores user roles, tasks and reports. The Flutter app surfaces three role-based portals — Admin, Supervisor and Cleaner — each subscribed to live data.

How It Works — End-to-End Flow

1

Sensor reads fill level

An ESP32 + ultrasonic sensor inside the bin measures fill % every few seconds and publishes it to Firebase Realtime Database.

2

App classifies status

The Flutter app categorises each bin as Low / Medium / High / Full using thresholds (30 / 60 / 90%).

3

Supervisor assigns task

When a bin hits High or Full, the supervisor assigns a collection task to the nearest cleaner with a priority level.

4

Cleaner is alerted

A high-priority full-screen Android notification (full_bins_channel / assigned_tasks_channel) wakes the cleaner's device — even on idle.

5

Task completed & logged

The cleaner empties the bin, marks the task complete in-app, the sensor confirms the new fill level, and the event is logged to Firestore for reporting.

6

Admin reviews analytics

Admins see live KPIs and historical reports — full-bin frequency, cleaner performance, hostel hot-spots — to plan resourcing.
